Hawaii March 27, 2026

The Big Island

After the hustle and bustle of Waikīkī and Honolulu, things slowed down a lot on the big island of Hawaiʻi. The big island is larger in area than all the other Hawaiian islands combined, but it's the second most-populated, so the pace of things felt a bit more relaxed to me there. Some interesting facts about Hawaiʻi are here on a state government page.

The big island is also the largest island in the United States, and it's growing, as volcanoes continue to erupt and lava adds to the land mass.

Here are some window seat-view photos taken during my flight from Honolulu to Hilo.

I arrived in Hilo at about 7 a.m., rented a car, and proceeded to drive around the island for the rest of the day. My first stop was Hawaiʻi Volcanoes National Park, which includes two of the world's most active volcanoes, Kīlauea and Mauna Loa, according to the National Parks Service.

Mauna Loa is about 13,700 feet above sea level, but from the bottom of the ocean to its summit, it's like 30,000 feet tall, making it taller than Mt. Everest.

The park is really neat, with lots of trails that bring you to very unique terrain. You can even take a trail out on the Kīlauea Iki Crater inside the Kīlauea caldera…

You can also drive down from the summits to the ocean, down to the Hōlei Sea Arch. It's a very curvy road to drive down, really fun. You drive through a massive lava field, so it's just a very unique environment to be in. And of course it's beautiful, like basically everywhere in Hawaiʻi.

After the volcanoes, I went to Papakōlea Beach, which the internet says is one of a very small number (2? 4?) of "green sand" beaches in the world. I got there a little late in the day and wasn't expecting it to be like a three-mile hike from the parking lot. There were locals with big 4x4s who would drive you to and from the beach, but I decided to hike it. And I was glad I did, it was a main highlight of my trip to Hawaiʻi. A really fun hike and then the beach was super neat. It was mostly cloudy, so not sure I got the full "green sand" effect, but you could definitely tell the sand had a unique quality.
