Japan April 8, 2026

Himeji & Kobe

Himeji

Another day trip took me to Himeji Castle. A lot of the castles in Japan are rebuilds of ones that burned down or were destroyed in other ways in the past, like that one in Kyoto, and the one in Osaka, which was rebuilt in the 1930s.

But the one in Himeji stands more or less as it was built in the very early 1600s. It's known as the White Heron castle because of its exterior paint job. One interesting thing about Himeji is that while it has never been destroyed like other castles, it has been dismantled — yes, dismantled — and put back together again as part of a restoration project.

1 / 8

It was really spectacular on a clear day with the cherry blossoms. And, you can walk through the main keep (you get a bag to put your shoes in and slippers if you need them) and other parts of the complex.

1 / 8

And there are lots of really beautiful gardens and areas with foliage, too. Himeji was really a highlight of the Japan trip.

On the way back to the train station I noticed this restaurant … The Bon Marché used to be a department store in the Pacific Northwest (which was apparently inspired by Le Bon Marché in Paris). Anyway, the name lives on.

Kobe

And I went to Kobe, although that wasn't really a day trip, I just stopped off there on the way back from Himeji. Kobe has a little Chinatown right near a train stop, so I got off there and walked around it and the nearby shōtengai and got some food.
